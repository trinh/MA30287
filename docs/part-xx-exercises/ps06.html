Q1. Non-dimensionalisation of the ocean model

Take the ocean model from (Equation 25.7). Re-scale the salinity and time via \[ \Delta S = [\Delta S]x \quad \text{and} \quad t = [t]t'. \] By choosing appropriate scales, show that the equation can be reduced to the form \[ \dot{x} = \lambda - |1 - x|x \tag{33.1}\] for the parameter \(\lambda\) which you should define. Note that the above quantity \(\dot{x}\) refers to differentiation in \(t'\).

Provide an intuitive interpretation of the parameter \(\lambda\).

Q2. Steady states of the reduced model

Consider now the reduced model (Equation 33.1) and solve for the steady-state solutions (or equilibrium solutions). Assume that \(\lambda > 0\) and consider the two cases of \(1 - x \geq 0\) and \(1 - x < 0\). Show that there are up to three steady states given by \[ \begin{align} x_1^* &= \frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{1 + 4 \lambda}), \\ x_{2,3}^* &= \frac{1}{2}(1 \pm \sqrt{1 - 4\lambda}), \end{align} \] and derive conditions for each point to exist.

Next, set \(x = x^* + y\) where \(x^*\) is one of the three possible steady-state solutions. Show that the perturbation is governed by \[ \dot{y} = \pm (2x^* - 1)y, \qquad x^* \lessgtr 1. \] Conclude that \(x_1^*\) is stable, \(x_2^*\) is unstable, and \(x_3^*\) is stable.

Q3. Bifurcation diagram for the reduced ocean model

Based on your work in Q2, plot a bifurcation diagram in the \((\lambda, x^*)\)-plane. Use solid and dashed curves to indicate stability.

Does the bifurcation diagram indicate the possibility of tipping points and hysteresis? By considering the interpretation of \(\lambda\) and \(x^*\), discuss the physical consequences of such behaviour as it relates to the Ocean model.

How does this bifurcation diagram compare to the typical bifurcation diagrams studied in the Energy Balance module, such as the one studied in Chapter 20 ?

Q4. Non-dimensionalisation of the Stommel box model

Consider the Stommel box model given in (Equation 26.1). Non-dimensionalise the model in order to produce the set of equations given in (Equation 26.2), repeated here: \[ \begin{align} \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\mathrm{d}t} &= \delta(1 - x) - |f(x, y)|x, \\ \frac{\mathrm{d}y}{\mathrm{d}t} &= 1 - y - |f(x, y)|y, \end{align} \tag{33.2}\] where we have introduced the function, \[ f(x, y; R, \lambda) = \frac{1}{\lambda}(Rx - y), \tag{33.3}\]

Provide a brief description of the physical interpretations of the parameters \(\lambda\),\(\delta\), and \(R\).

Q4. Investigations for \(R\)

Using the script available on the Jupyter hub, investigate the behaviour of the system (Equation 33.2) under changing values of \(R\) and fixed values of the other parameters. Based on your intuition, what do you expect to be the features of the bifurcation diagram, as shown in the \((R, f^*)\)-plane?

Q5. Alternative scalings of the Stommel box model

It is possible to scale the problem differently, and this may allow for simpler analysis. Consider the set of equations (Equation 33.2). Write instead \[ x = [x] s, \qquad y = [y] \theta, \qquad t = [t] t', \] Choose the correct scalings, \([x]\), \([y]\), and \([t]\), so that we obtain a re-scaled version: \[ \begin{align} \frac{\mathrm{d}s}{\mathrm{d}t'} &= 1 - (\epsilon + |q|) s, \\ \frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{\mathrm{d}t'} &= 1 - (\mu + |q|)\theta, \end{align} \] where now the flow term is given by \[ q = \kappa(-\theta + \tilde{R}s). \] In addition to the choice of scalings, you will need to describe how the new parameters, \(\epsilon\), \(\mu\), \(\kappa\), and \(\tilde{R}\), are related to the older parameters.
